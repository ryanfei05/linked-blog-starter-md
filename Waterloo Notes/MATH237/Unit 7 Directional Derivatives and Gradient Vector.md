**Geometric Representation**
- when applying directional derivative
	- (x,y) usually position
	- f(x,y) is some physical quantity like temperature, height
	- represents rate of change with respect to distant
- slope of the tangent line to surface z = f(x,y) in a specific direction defined by u

**Greatest Rate of Change**
![[Pasted image 20260331170035.png]]
- directional derivative of f and (a,b) is largest value in same direction of gradient vector, and equals the magnitude of the gradient vector
- applies in any dimension
